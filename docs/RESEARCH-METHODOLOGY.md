# Research Methodology

> Cross-Border Payment Cost Research — 15 Corridors to China
> April 2026

## Scope

This research covers the total cost of sending money from 15 source countries to China, comparing domestic banks, fintechs, and WorldFirst across 7 cost dimensions. Approximately 80 providers were researched in total.

## Source Countries

UK, Australia, Turkey, Vietnam, Nigeria, Pakistan, Germany, Bangladesh, France, Netherlands, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Thailand, Indonesia, India.

## The Seven Cost Dimensions

For every provider on every corridor:

1. **FX Markup** — Difference between the provider's offered rate and the mid-market (interbank) rate, expressed as a percentage. Mid-market sourced from XE.com and relevant central bank (BoE, ECB, RBA, TCMB, SBV, CBN, SBP, BB, CBUAE, SAMA, BOT, BI, RBI).

2. **Transfer Fees** — Flat or percentage-based fee per transaction. Documented by channel (online, branch, phone) where data is available.

3. **Intermediary / Receiving Fees** — Correspondent bank charges in the SWIFT chain. Whether the provider uses SWIFT or direct local payment rails. SHA/OUR/BEN options where documented.

4. **Account / Maintenance Fees** — Monthly fees, setup fees, inactivity fees, minimum balances.

5. **Speed** — Standard transfer time in business days. Express options. Same-day CNY capability.

6. **Safety / Regulation** — Regulatory status (FCA, APRA, BaFin, BDDK, SBV, CBN, SBP, BB, CBUAE, SAMA, BOT, OJK/BI, RBI). Fund safeguarding or deposit insurance. Parent company.

7. **Limitations** — Maximum transfer amounts, daily/monthly limits, supported currencies, CNY holding capability, forward contracts, API/batch payments.

## Data Source Hierarchy

1. **Provider's own website** — rate calculator, published fee schedule, terms and conditions (gold standard)
2. **Independent comparison sites** — Wise comparison, Monito, Exiap, KeyCurrency, Rutland FX, MoneyTransferComparison, thecurrencyshop.com.au, moneytransfer.com.au, finder.com.au
3. **Financial publications** — Finextra, Wise 2024 transparency study, BBVA Research, Deutsche Bank research
4. **Central bank data** — PBOC, ECB, BoE, RBA, TCMB, SBV, CBN, SBP, BB, SAMA, BOT, BI, RBI reference rates
5. **Trade statistics** — ABS, Destatis, HMRC, SWIFT RMB Tracker, World Bank Remittance Prices, City of London/PBOC Annual Report
6. **Industry estimates** — Used only when sources 1-5 produce nothing. Always flagged as "estimated."

## Confidence Ratings

| Rating | Definition | Display |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Published | From the provider's own website, rate calculator, or fee schedule | Provider-sourced |
| Third-Party | From independent comparison sites that actively sample rates | Comparison-verified |
| Estimated | Derived from regional averages, industry reports, or pattern matching | Industry estimate |
| Unavailable | Could not find reliable data from any source | Data not available |

## Comparison Methodology

- **Transfer amount:** $10,000 USD equivalent (converted to local currency at mid-market rate)
- **Account type:** Standard retail or SME online banking (not Premier, Private, or Corporate)
- **Payment method:** Online bank transfer (not branch, phone, or card)
- **Pricing:** Standard rates without negotiation or relationship discounts
- **WorldFirst rates:** Maximum published FX ceiling per corridor from the WorldFirst Product Handbook (not typical or negotiated)

## Currency Composition Research

Before provider comparison, each corridor's settlement currency mix was researched:
- Source: SWIFT RMB Tracker, central bank data, ABS/Destatis/HMRC trade statistics, City of London/PBOC reports, academic invoicing research (Gopinath et al.)
- Rule: If any currency exceeds 20% settlement share, it receives dedicated analysis (USD Route / CNY Direct tabs)
- The USD route includes three cost layers: sender's FX conversion, Chinese bank's USD→CNY conversion, and the FX risk buffer embedded in USD-quoted prices

## Compliance Framework

All outputs follow UK BPR (Business Protection from Misleading Marketing Regulations 2008) and FCA COBS 4:
- No misleading data (ranges with stated typical values)
- Like-for-like comparisons (same amount, corridor, delivery method)
- Every claim backed by verifiable evidence
- No denigration (factual language, never attribute intent to banks)
- WorldFirst shown at maximum published rate, not typical
- "Approximately" or "estimated" before all comparative figures
- Disclaimer present on every report

## What This Research Does Not Cover

- Negotiated corporate rates (may be significantly lower for high-volume clients)
- Forward contract pricing
- Premium / private banking tiers
- Import duties, VAT, customs brokerage
- Letter of credit costs (except where LC is the standard payment method, e.g., Bangladesh)
- Informal / hawala channels (noted as context but not priced)
