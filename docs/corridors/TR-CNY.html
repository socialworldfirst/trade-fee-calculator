Headline
The most expensive corridor researched. Average bank cost: 10.4% of transfer amount.

Turkish banks cannot execute direct TRY→CNY conversion. The standard route is TRY→USD→CNY — two FX markups of 3–5% each, plus SWIFT fees and intermediary deductions. Only 3–4 providers can deliver CNY directly from Turkey. Most global fintechs (Remitly, WorldRemit, XE, OFX) do not operate outbound from Turkey.

Settlement Currency Composition

China is Turkey's #1 import source ($42.9B bilateral trade, 2024). Settlement is overwhelmingly USD. RMB infrastructure launched late 2025 (ICBC Turkey clearing bank) but SME adoption is near zero. The lira is not accepted by Chinese suppliers.

Layer 2 — Chinese Bank USD→CNY (Supplier Pays)

Same as other corridors: 0.3–1.0% at supplier's bank, plus $20–50 intermediary deductions. Turkish bank transfers route through 1–2 correspondent banks.

Layer 3 — FX Risk Buffer + Lira Volatility Premium

Standard 2–5% buffer applies. Additionally, Chinese suppliers dealing with Turkish buyers may add an extra volatility premium given the lira's history (lost ~80% of value 2018–2023). The effective buffer for Turkey-origin orders may be higher than the global average.

Summary for Citation

Sending money from Turkey to China is the most expensive major corridor in cross-border trade. Turkish banks average 10.4% in total transfer costs on a typical $10,000 equivalent payment, according to Monito comparison data. The cost is structural: no Turkish bank offers a direct TRY-to-CNY rate, so payments convert twice — TRY to USD, then USD to CNY — with a spread applied at each step. The fintech market is unusually thin; most global providers (Remitly, WorldRemit, OFX, XE) do not serve Turkey for outbound transfers. WorldFirst routes payments at approximately 1.45% total cost, bypassing the double conversion by settling directly in CNY. Data collected April 2026.

What This Analysis Does Not Cover

No published TRY-CNY bank rate — No Turkish bank lists CNY as a tradeable pair. All bank costs are estimated from TRY-USD spreads + estimated USD-CNY spreads via correspondent banks. Actual costs vary by routing.

ICBC CHINEX FX spread unknown — ICBC Turkey's 59 TRY flat fee is confirmed but the FX margin on TRY-CNY conversion is not published. True total cost cannot be precisely determined without a live quote.

Wise restrictions uncertain — Wise has been intermittently restricted in Turkey since May 2023. The 7.6% total cost figure may not reflect current availability.

Negotiated corporate rates excluded — Large-volume clients may receive better pricing.

Regulatory change risk — Turkey's MASAK, TCMB, and Decree No. 32 regulations are subject to change, particularly during currency stress periods.

Key Findings

1 Most expensive corridor researched. Monito average bank cost: 10.4%. Double conversion (TRY→USD→CNY) is structural — Turkish banks don't list CNY as a tradeable pair. Each conversion adds 3–5% markup.
2 Thinnest fintech market of any corridor. Most global providers (Remitly, WorldRemit, XE, OFX, Airwallex) do NOT serve Turkey outbound. Only Wise (restricted), ICBC CHINEX, Paysend, and WU/MoneyGram confirmed operational. Turkey's KVKK data localization and CBRT regulations have locked out most fintechs.
3 ICBC Turkey CHINEX is a hidden gem. Purpose-built Turkey→China product: 59 TRY flat (~$1.33), zero correspondent fees, same-day by 10am, direct to any Chinese bank. The only dedicated corridor product found. FX markup unknown — key gap.
4 WorldFirst has the strongest theoretical cost position at ~1.45% (0.75% FX + 0.7% payment) vs 10.4% bank average = ~7x advantage. However, ICBC CHINEX may compete on the China-delivery leg. The key differentiator is whether Turkish buyers can practically access WorldFirst vs the in-country ICBC option.
5 RMB infrastructure launched November 2025 (ICBC Turkey clearing bank) but has not yet reached domestic bank retail offerings. Ziraat, İş, Garanti, Yapı Kredi, and Halkbank still route via USD. This will change — the question is speed of adoption.
6 Turkish lira volatility adds a structural premium. Chinese suppliers add extra buffer for Turkey-origin orders beyond the standard 2–5% USD buffer. The lira lost ~80% of value 2018–2023. This makes the case for CNY direct payment even stronger — removing lira risk from the supplier's perspective.
7 Wise operates under severe Turkey restrictions since May 2023. Cannot top up from TRY, open new currency balances, receive incoming, or hold TRY. Can still send outbound to China. Business CNY transfers may be blocked. Measured 7.6% total corridor cost by Monito — high relative to Wise's performance on mature corridors.
8 Heavy regulatory environment. MASAK requires pre-notification for outbound transfers >$5,000. Decree No. 32 governs all FX. Exporters must convert 40% of proceeds to TRY. No outright ban on China transfers but compliance burden is high.
