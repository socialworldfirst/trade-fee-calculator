03 — Key Findings 10 findings • research grade
  • 01 Thailand has the strongest RMB infrastructure in mainland Southeast Asia. ICBC (Thai) has served as the official PBOC-designated RMB clearing bank in Bangkok since January 2015. The PBOC–BOT bilateral currency swap (CNY 70B / THB 370B, ~$9.8B) was renewed in August 2025 for a further five years. This institutional plumbing enables direct THB→CNY conversion without mandatory USD intermediation — a structural advantage over corridors like Vietnam and Bangladesh.
  • 02 Wise will launch outbound THB transfers on May 19, 2026. This is the single most significant upcoming disruption to this corridor. Once live, Wise will offer THB→CNY at approximately 1.05% total fee using mid-market rates, fully regulated by the Bank of Thailand. However, all transfers must originate in THB — no foreign currency balances can be sent directly, meaning double conversion for non-THB holdings. This will compress bank margins and challenge WorldFirst on small-to-medium personal and business transfers.
  • 03 PromptPay–China cross-border QR payments launched October 30, 2025. The Bank of Thailand linked PromptPay with Alipay, UnionPay, and WeChat Pay. Six Thai banks participate: Bangkok Bank, Krungthai, Kasikornbank, ICBC Thai, SCB, and BAAC. As of April 2026, this covers retail and tourism — Chinese tourists scanning Thai QR codes. Inbound QR transaction value reached THB 397M in September 2025, up 55% YoY. Not yet available for B2B trade, but signals deepening payment integration.
  • 04 Thailand's trade deficit with China ballooned to $45.4 billion in 2024 (+23.8% YoY). China is Thailand's largest trading partner. Bilateral trade reached an estimated $153 billion in 2025. Thailand imports overwhelmingly electronics, machinery, and EV components from China — meaning massive net payment demand flows from Thai importers to Chinese suppliers, squarely in WorldFirst's direction.
  • 05 SCB’s Thunes partnership enables instant CNY transfers via the SCB Easy app. Siam Commercial Bank partnered with Thunes (a Ripple-affiliated payments network) to offer instant international remittance to China in Chinese yuan. Transfer fee starts at THB 199 (promotional: THB 99). This is the first Thai bank to offer near-real-time China transfers — a direct competitor to WorldFirst on speed, though FX markup remains opaque.
  • 06 DeeMoney is the leading local fintech for China remittance — licensed by the Bank of Thailand, partnered directly with Alipay and WeChat Pay for wallet-to-wallet delivery. Flat fee: THB 150–250 + VAT per transaction plus FX markup. Competitive for small personal remittances (especially Chinese nationals in Thailand sending home). Not built for B2B trade volumes or supplier payments.
  • 07 Thai banks charge THB 300–1,050 per SWIFT transaction depending on who bears the correspondent fees. Bangkok Bank: THB 300–400 (sender only) or THB 1,050–1,150 (all-inclusive). Kasikornbank: THB 250 promotional for CNY via partner channel. These flat fees are modest on large transfers but add 1–3% on top of FX markup for sub-$5,000 transfers.
  • 08 Bangkok Bank offers a discounted FX spread for CNY of THB 0.05 per unit (compared to THB 0.15 for USD), suggesting the CNY spread as a percentage is actually tighter than USD at current exchange rate levels. However, this Bualuang Exclusive rate is only available to premium clients. Standard retail FX markup is estimated at 1.5–3%.
  • 09 Canton Fair actively promotes to Thai buyers. Thailand is one of the largest ASEAN buyer contingents at the Canton Fair, with dedicated tour operators (EDC Travel Plus, operating since the 106th edition), Thai consulate representation in Guangzhou, and 14+ Thai exhibitors in the International Pavilion. This creates a concentrated pool of Thai businesses making regular supplier payments to China.
  • 10 BOT foreign exchange regulations are relatively liberal compared to other ASEAN economies. Thailand uses a managed float with no formal band. Residents can transfer up to $50,000/year without restriction; business transfers linked to trade have no cap if documentation is provided. No mandatory LC or complex documentation requirements for standard imports. This regulatory environment allows fintechs and non-bank providers to compete more freely than in Vietnam or Bangladesh.

SCB offers instant CNY delivery via Thunes/Ripple network — genuine real-time settlement. WorldFirst offers same-day via CNAPS. Both significantly faster than standard SWIFT (1–3 days). Choose SCB for single instant transfers; choose WorldFirst for ongoing trade payment volume.
09 — Frequently Asked Questions 8 questions • Thailand-specific
What is the cheapest way to send money from Thailand to China? +
For business trade payments: WorldFirst at approximately 1.9% total cost (up to 1.5% FX markup plus 0.40% China payment fee). For personal transfers after May 2026: Wise at approximately 1.05% with mid-market rates. For API-integrated businesses: Airwallex at 0.5–1%. Thai bank alternatives range from 2% (Bangkok Bank best case) to 4% (Krungsri standard). ICBC Thai offers competitive direct THB-CNY rates at approximately 1.5–2.5% but serves a niche clientele.
Can I use Wise to send money from Thailand to China? +
Not yet. As of April 2026, Wise cannot send THB outbound from Thailand. Starting May 19, 2026, Wise will operate through a locally incorporated Thai entity regulated by the Bank of Thailand and will enable outbound THB transfers for the first time. Key restriction: all transfers must originate in THB — you cannot send USD or other foreign currency balances directly abroad. For CNY payments, you will convert THB directly to CNY at Wise's mid-market rate plus approximately 1.05% fee. This will make Wise highly competitive for personal and SME transfers to China.
Does Thailand have a currency swap agreement with China? +
Yes. The People's Bank of China and the Bank of Thailand maintain a bilateral currency swap agreement worth 70 billion yuan (370 billion baht, approximately $9.8 billion USD). Most recently renewed in August 2025 for a five-year term. This swap provides institutional RMB liquidity to Thai banks, enables direct THB-CNY conversion, and contributes to financial market stability. Thailand is one of seven countries where ICBC operates a PBOC-designated RMB clearing bank.
What is the PromptPay-China cross-border QR payment? +
Launched October 30, 2025, the Bank of Thailand linked Thailand's national instant payment system (PromptPay) with three Chinese payment providers: Alipay, UnionPay, and WeChat Pay. Six Thai banks participate: Bangkok Bank, Krungthai, Kasikornbank, ICBC Thai, SCB, and BAAC. Chinese tourists in Thailand can scan local PromptPay QR codes to pay in RMB using their Chinese wallet apps. As of April 2026, this covers retail and tourism only — not B2B trade. Inbound QR transaction value reached THB 397 million in September 2025 (+55% YoY), reflecting growing adoption.
Is ICBC Thailand the RMB clearing bank? +
Yes. ICBC (Thai) Public Company Limited was designated by the PBOC as the official RMB clearing bank in Bangkok in January 2015. This enables commercial banks in Thailand and the broader ASEAN region to open RMB accounts directly with ICBC Thai, facilitating direct RMB settlement without USD intermediation. ICBC Thai is the largest Chinese bank in Thailand, having acquired ACL Bank in 2010. It is one of seven PBOC-designated RMB clearing banks operated by ICBC globally (others: Singapore, Luxembourg, Canada, Qatar, Argentina, Russia).
What does DeeMoney offer for China transfers? +
DeeMoney is a Thai-licensed fintech (regulated by the Bank of Thailand) offering international remittance to 89 countries including China. For China specifically, DeeMoney partners directly with Alipay and WeChat Pay, enabling wallet-to-wallet delivery without needing Chinese bank account details. Flat fee: approximately THB 150–250 plus 7% VAT per transaction, plus FX markup of approximately 1–2%. Best for personal remittances — especially Chinese nationals in Thailand sending money home. Not designed for large B2B trade payments.
What are Thailand's foreign exchange regulations for outbound payments? +
Thailand's FX regime is relatively liberal by ASEAN standards. The baht operates on a managed float — the BOT intervenes only to moderate excessive volatility, with no formal trading band. For individuals, outbound transfers up to $50,000 per year require no special approval. For businesses, trade-linked payments have no cap provided proper documentation is submitted. Thai companies can invest abroad with no restriction if they hold 10%+ in the foreign entity. Recent BOT reforms have further liberalized outbound remittance for certain purposes including lending to unaffiliated entities and overseas property purchases.
How does SCB's instant China transfer work? +
Siam Commercial Bank partnered with Thunes (a Ripple-affiliated global payment network) to offer instant international transfers via the SCB Easy app. The service supports 26 countries and 17 currencies including Chinese yuan. Transfer fee starts at THB 199 per transaction. Settlement to China is real-time via the Thunes network, making it significantly faster than standard SWIFT (1–3 business days). This is currently the fastest bank-to-bank payment option from Thailand to China. FX markup is applied on top of the flat fee but is not separately disclosed.

Thailand occupies a uniquely favorable position among ASEAN corridors for China trade payments. It has the most developed RMB infrastructure in mainland Southeast Asia: a PBOC-designated clearing bank (ICBC Thai, since 2015), an active bilateral currency swap ($9.8B, renewed 2025), and a live cross-border QR payment link between PromptPay and China's three major payment platforms. This institutional plumbing means Thai importers have more options for direct THB→CNY settlement than their Vietnamese, Bangladeshi, or Pakistani counterparts.

The competitive landscape is richer here than in most corridors studied. Five major banks compete alongside local fintechs (DeeMoney), global fintechs (Airwallex, and Wise from May 2026), and WorldFirst. SCB's Thunes/Ripple-powered instant transfer is a genuinely innovative bank product not found elsewhere. The imminent Wise launch in May 2026 will introduce mid-market rate pricing to this corridor for the first time, compressing margins across all providers.

Thailand's $45.4 billion trade deficit with China creates enormous net payment demand flowing from Thai importers to Chinese exporters. The country's relatively liberal FX regulations — no formal band, no mandatory LC for standard imports, no cap on documented trade payments — make it easier for fintechs and non-bank providers to operate. Canton Fair is actively marketed to Thai buyers, and dedicated tour operators have organized Thai delegations for over 30 editions of the fair.

11 — Data Limitations Methodology notes • Next review: October 2026
  • Bank FX markups are estimated ranges based on published indicative rates, comparison service data (Wise, XE), and market intelligence. Thai banks do not publicly disclose exact FX margin schedules — actual rates are negotiated per client relationship, transaction size, and currency pair.
  • Bangkok Bank's THB 0.05/unit CNY spread is for Bualuang Exclusive premium clients only. Standard retail spread is wider and not publicly documented. The percentage markup depends on the exchange rate level and varies intraday.
  • Wise pricing (~1.05% fee) is based on pre-launch announcements and Wise's standard THB-to-CNY fee calculator. Actual pricing post-May 19, 2026 may differ. The THB-only outbound restriction is confirmed by Wise and the BOT regulatory framework.
  • DeeMoney FX markup is estimated from user reports and promotional materials. The company does not publish a specific percentage markup schedule. Wallet delivery rates (Alipay/WeChat) may differ from bank account delivery rates.
  • ICBC Thai pricing is the least documented provider in this report. As a niche Chinese bank in Thailand, published rate information is minimal. Estimates are based on comparable ICBC operations in other ASEAN markets and ICBC Thai's structural advantage as the RMB clearing bank.
  • Airwallex availability assumes a formal business entity with access to the Airwallex platform. Pricing is based on published business account rates. Not available as a self-serve retail product in Thailand.
  • SCB Thunes pricing includes a disclosed flat fee (THB 199–399) but the FX markup component is not separately published. Total cost is estimated from comparison service data.
  • WorldFirst costs use the maximum published FX ceiling from the WorldFirst Product Handbook (SMT tier) — not typical or negotiated rates. China payment fee of 0.40% applies at high volume; standard payment fees are $1 (local) to $5 (international).
  • Currency composition (USD ~70–80%, CNY ~10–15%) is estimated from SWIFT data, BOT statistics, and PBOC internationalization reports. Thailand-specific breakdown is not separately published.
  • Data collected April 2026. Exchange rates, regulatory requirements, and provider pricing are subject to change. The Wise launch on May 19, 2026 will materially alter the competitive landscape. Next scheduled review: October 2026.
