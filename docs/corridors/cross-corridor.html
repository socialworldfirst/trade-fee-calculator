Key Findings

1 Turkey is the most expensive corridor at 10.4% average bank cost. The double conversion (TRY→USD→CNY) is structural — no Turkish bank lists CNY as a tradeable pair. Most global fintechs don't serve Turkey outbound.
2 Australia has the largest bank-fintech spread. AU banks charge 8.8–10.4% specifically for CNY — 2 to 4 times their own USD rates. WorldFirst at 0.67% is 13–15x cheaper. No other corridor shows this magnitude of differential.
3 ING Netherlands (0.85%) is the most competitive traditional bank across all 15 corridors. It is the only bank that approaches fintech pricing. WorldFirst at 1.15% is more expensive than ING on this corridor.
4 WorldFirst is not the cheapest in 4 of 15 corridors (Nigeria, Bangladesh, Netherlands, India). In Nigeria, emerging Africa-China fintechs offer 1–3%. In India, WF's P1 pricing (4.5–6%) exceeds bank rates (2.5–5%). These findings are stated in each corridor report.
5 RMB adoption varies 100x across corridors. Pakistan settles 19.1% of China trade in RMB. Bangladesh settles less than 1%. The UK aggregate is 43.4% but SME share is approximately 20–25%. Infrastructure exists in most corridors (swap lines, clearing banks) but SME adoption lags.
6 Vietnam and Bangladesh are fintech deserts for outbound payments. Wise cannot send from VND or BDT. Most global fintechs serve these countries for inbound remittances only. WorldFirst's competitive position is strongest where fintech alternatives are fewest.
7 The USD route is the default everywhere. Even in corridors with RMB infrastructure (Thailand, Indonesia, Pakistan), 65–90% of trade still settles in USD. The double conversion (local currency→USD→CNY) and the supplier's FX risk buffer (2–5%) remain the dominant cost drivers.
8 Airwallex is WorldFirst's most consistent global competitor. Present in UK, Australia, Germany, France, Netherlands, Thailand, Indonesia — always at approximately 0.5% FX. Business-only with local CNY eligibility restrictions in every market.
9 1688 World Pay is WorldFirst's uncontested feature. No bank, no fintech, no exchange house offers direct checkout on 1688.com in any corridor. For importers sourcing from China's largest wholesale platform, WorldFirst is the only option.

Summary for Citation

The cost of sending $10,000 to China varies dramatically by origin country. Turkey is the most expensive corridor, with banks averaging approximately 10.4% in total transfer costs due to a structural double conversion (TRY to USD to CNY). Australian banks charge 8.8–10.4% specifically for CNY transfers — 2 to 4 times their own USD rates. UK banks cost approximately £255–410 per £10,000, while German banks cost approximately €300–700. At the lower end, French and Dutch banks offer approximately €150–509 depending on the institution, with ING Netherlands at 0.85% being the most competitive traditional bank found across all 15 corridors researched. WorldFirst's cost ranges from approximately 0.5% (UK) to 2.25% (UAE/Saudi Arabia), depending on the corridor's pricing tier. In four corridors — Nigeria, Bangladesh, India, and the Netherlands — WorldFirst is not the cheapest option. This research covers 15 origin countries and approximately 80 providers, with data sourced from bank fee schedules, independent comparison platforms (Wise, Monito, Exiap, KeyCurrency), central bank reference rates, and regulatory filings. Data collected April 2026.

Methodology

Each corridor researched across 7 cost dimensions: FX markup, transfer fees, intermediary/receiving fees, account costs, speed, safety/regulation, and limitations. Providers include domestic banks (top 3–5 by market share per country), fintechs (Wise, Airwallex, OFX, XE, Remitly, plus regional specialists), and WorldFirst.

WorldFirst costs use the maximum published FX ceiling from the WorldFirst Product Handbook — not typical or negotiated rates. Bank costs use standard retail/SME online banking rates. Negotiated corporate rates, forward contract pricing, and premium banking tiers are excluded.

Currency composition researched per corridor using SWIFT RMB Tracker, central bank data, ABS/Destatis/HMRC trade statistics, and industry reports. Settlement currency split determines whether reports include USD Route and CNY Direct comparison.
