AE-CNY Corridor

Cross-Border Payment Cost Research — United Arab Emirates to China
3 April 2026 Mid-market: 1 AED = 1.88 CNY 1 USD = 3.6725 AED (pegged) 12 Providers
Headline
The Middle East’s deepest China corridor. $101.8B bilateral trade (2024), USD-pegged currency, and dedicated RMB clearing infrastructure.

The UAE is unique among corridors researched: a USD-pegged currency eliminates FX volatility risk, a deep exchange house network competes with banks on price, and two PBOC-designated RMB clearing banks (Agricultural Bank of China since 2016, First Abu Dhabi Bank since 2025) provide direct CNY settlement rails. Bank costs run 3–6% but competitive exchange houses and fintechs push the best available rate below 1%. Over 60% of Chinese exports to the UAE are re-exported to Africa and the Middle East, making Dubai the region’s primary China trade gateway.

Settlement Currency Composition

The UAE is China’s largest Arab trading partner. Bilateral trade hit $101.8B in 2024 — an 800-fold increase since 1984. The AED’s peg to USD at 3.6725 means Chinese suppliers treat AED-origin payments identically to USD. RMB settlement is growing fast: cross-border yuan payments between China and UAE reached 864 billion yuan in the first nine months of 2025 (+20% YoY).

Cost Analysis by Currency Route

The standard route for most UAE businesses. Banks convert AED→USD (near-zero cost due to the peg) then send USD via SWIFT to China. The supplier’s Chinese bank converts USD→CNY. The peg eliminates the first-leg FX risk that plagues corridors like Turkey and Nigeria, making the total cost significantly lower than volatile-currency corridors.

The AED/USD peg at 3.6725 means the first-leg FX conversion is negligible for major banks — typically 0.1–0.3% for standard accounts. The real cost is in the commission/transfer fee layer plus the supplier’s USD→CNY conversion. Emirates NBD charges 1.99% commission on international transfers, which is the largest cost component.

Layer 2 — Chinese Bank USD→CNY (Supplier Pays)

Same as other corridors: 0.3–1.0% at supplier’s bank, plus $15–50 in intermediary/correspondent deductions. UAE bank transfers to China typically route through 0–1 correspondent banks (fewer hops than emerging market corridors due to deep banking relationships).

Layer 3 — FX Risk Buffer

Standard 2–5% buffer applies. However, AED-origin payments carry lower perceived risk than volatile currencies. Chinese suppliers familiar with UAE trade (and there are 8,000+ Chinese companies in-country) are less likely to apply the upper end of the buffer. The peg effectively neutralizes sender-side FX risk.

Banks (5)

Emirates NBD — Largest UAE bank by assets. 1.99% commission on international transfers. DirectRemit AED 26.25 per transfer (since Sep 2025). FX margin 0.2–4.6%. Correspondent bank fees AED 40–400 additional. AED transfers outside UAE: AED 105 to CBUAE. CBUAE regulated.
First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB) — Largest by market cap. Second PBOC-designated RMB clearing bank in UAE (Oct 2025). First MENA bank on CIPS. FX margin 0.2–5%. Total cost 0.5–6%. Elite/Private: 1 free transfer/month. Shanghai branch operational. First MENA bank on China’s NFRA Advisory Council. CBUAE regulated.
ADCB — Third-largest UAE bank. AED 21 per transfer (1st/month free). FX margin 0.2–5%. Correspondent fees AED 40–400. 1–2 day delivery. Digital and branch channels. CBUAE regulated.
Mashreq — Pioneer in UAE digital banking. Strong online/mobile transfer experience. Standard SWIFT routing to China. Competitive on smaller amounts. CBUAE regulated.
Dubai Islamic Bank — Largest Islamic bank in UAE. Sharia-compliant international transfers. Standard SWIFT routing. Competitive on GCC corridors but less specialized on China. CBUAE regulated.

Fintechs (3)

Remitly — Dominant on AED→CNY corridor. Cheapest provider in 99.3% of Monito searches (past 3 months). Measured total cost: <1% (sometimes negative on promotional rates). Delivers via Alipay, WeChat, bank deposit. AED 0 fee on standard transfers. Personal transfers only. New customer promo: free first transfer + promotional rate on first AED 4,000.
Wise — Fully operational from UAE (unlike Turkey restrictions). Mid-market rate + variable fee ~0.5–1.5%. Delivers via Alipay, WeChat, UnionPay, bank transfer. 74% instant delivery. Business and personal. Up to 50,000 CNY per transfer; annual recipient limits 300,000–600,000 CNY.
Paysend — UAE confirmed as sending country. $1.99 flat fee. Delivers via Alipay/WeChat. Fast (minutes). FX markup unknown but competitive. Worth investigating for small amounts.

What This Analysis Does Not Cover

Exchange house FX markups not precisely measured — Al Ansari, Lulu, and UAE Exchange do not publish rate sheets online. Markups estimated from Monito aggregates and industry norms. Actual costs vary by branch, time of day, and relationship.

FAB CNY clearing rates unknown — FAB’s new role as RMB clearing bank (Oct 2025) may enable competitive direct AED→CNY rates for corporate clients, but these are not publicly available.

Remitly personal only — Remitly’s market-leading rates are for personal transfers. Business/trade payments are not supported. WorldFirst’s value prop is B2B trade, not personal remittance.

Negotiated corporate rates excluded — Large-volume clients at any provider may receive better pricing.

Re-export payment flows not isolated — The $101.8B trade figure includes goods re-exported onward. The payment corridor is larger than bilateral consumption trade.
