03 — Key Findings 10 findings • research grade
  • 01 Indonesia holds the largest PBOC bilateral currency swap in Southeast Asia. The PBOC and Bank Indonesia renewed their swap in January 2025 at CNY 400 billion (IDR 878 trillion, ~$55.8 billion) — valid for five years. This is significantly larger than Thailand’s CNY 70B or Vietnam’s nonexistent swap. First signed in 2009, the agreement provides deep institutional RMB liquidity for ASEAN’s largest economy.
  • 02 The Local Currency Settlement (LCS) framework enables direct IDR-CNY conversion, but adoption lags infrastructure. Launched September 2021 and upgraded to the “LCT Framework” covering all Balance of Payments items in May 2025, eight Indonesian banks are designated ACCD: BCA, Mandiri, BNI, ICBC Indonesia, BOC Jakarta, CCB Indonesia, Danamon, and Maybank Indonesia. Cross-border CNY settlement under LCS reached CNY 16.2 billion ($2.3B) in 2024, up 24% YoY. Despite the plumbing, the vast majority of trade still settles in USD due to deeper liquidity.
  • 03 Indonesia has the richest domestic fintech ecosystem in ASEAN for outbound transfers. Wise, Flip (powered by Wise Platform), TopRemit, and Transfez all serve outbound IDR transfers to China. Flip Globe alone reaches Indonesia’s massive consumer base with 60+ country coverage and flat IDR 76,000 fees. This contrasts starkly with Vietnam (Wise cannot send VND) or Bangladesh (zero fintechs outbound). Indonesian importers have real, regulated alternatives to banks.
  • 04 Indonesian banks charge IDR 400,000–800,000 total per SWIFT transaction before FX markup. BCA: IDR 50,000 telex + $20 full-amount fee. Mandiri: $25 (USD) or $30 (other currencies). BNI: IDR 35,000 SWIFT + $5–50 commission + $25 correspondent. These fees are modest on large transfers but devastating on small amounts — adding 2.5–5% on sub-$2,000 transfers.
  • 05 QRIS-China cross-border QR payment pilot launched September 2025. Under PBOC-BI guidance, UnionPay International piloted the linkage between Indonesia’s QRIS (40+ million merchants) and Alipay/WeChat Pay. Chinese tourists pay at QRIS merchants; Indonesian users from 22 payment apps pay at pilot merchants in China. Settlement in local currencies. As of April 2026, retail-only — not B2B trade.
  • 06 Bilateral trade reached ~$148 billion in 2024, with Indonesia running a ~$10.5 billion deficit with China. China has been Indonesia’s largest trading partner for over a decade. Indonesia exports nickel, iron/steel, coal, and palm oil; imports machinery, electronics, vehicles, and plastics. The deficit widened to an estimated $22 billion in 2025, driven by surging Chinese EV and machinery imports. This net payment flow creates massive demand for IDR→CNY transfers.
  • 07 Bank of China Jakarta branch provides direct RMB banking services. BOC (Hong Kong) operates a Jakarta branch since 2003 offering cross-border RMB trade settlement, deposits, loans, remittances, and FX products. BOC Jakarta has processed over CNY 129 billion in clearing in a single half-year and brought CNY 2.7 billion in banknotes to the Indonesian market. ICBC Indonesia and CCB Indonesia also operate — three Chinese bank options.
  • 08 BI requires underlying transaction evidence for FX purchases exceeding $25,000 per month. Indonesia’s FX regime is a managed float with no formal band. Business trade payments linked to documented imports have no hard cap, but documentation is more stringent than Thailand. Since February 2025, natural resource exporters must retain 100% of export proceeds domestically for 12 months — indirectly tightening USD liquidity.
  • 09 Canton Fair is actively marketed to Indonesian buyers. Indonesia is one of the largest ASEAN buyer contingents at the Canton Fair, with dedicated travel agencies running “paket Canton Fair,” consulate presence in Guangzhou, and established importing communities in Jakarta, Surabaya, and Medan. The 1688.com marketplace is well-known among Indonesian resellers, making WorldFirst’s 1688 World Pay integration directly relevant.
  • 10 BCA offers direct IDR-CNY transfers via the LCS channel. As an ACCD-designated bank, BCA provides outward remittance settled in CNY or IDR through the Local Currency Transaction scheme — bypassing USD intermediation. Available via branch, myBCA app, and KlikBCA Bisnis. The FX spread on the direct IDR-CNY pair remains wider than IDR-USD due to lower liquidity, but the infrastructure advantage is real.

09 — Frequently Asked Questions 8 questions • Indonesia-specific
What is the cheapest way to send money from Indonesia to China?+
For business trade payments: WorldFirst at approximately 1.9% total cost. For personal transfers: Wise at approximately 1.1% using mid-market rates, or Flip Globe at IDR 76,000 flat fee. For API-integrated businesses: Airwallex at 0.5–1%. Indonesian bank alternatives range from 2.5% (BCA/Mandiri via LCS) to 5% (BRI standard SWIFT). BOC Jakarta offers competitive direct IDR-CNY rates at approximately 1.5–2.5% but serves a niche clientele.
Can I send IDR directly to CNY without using USD?+
Yes. Indonesia and China launched a Local Currency Settlement (LCS) framework in September 2021, enabling direct IDR-CNY conversion. Eight Indonesian banks are designated Appointed Cross Currency Dealers (ACCD): BCA, Bank Mandiri, BNI, ICBC Indonesia, Bank of China Jakarta, CCB Indonesia, Danamon, and Maybank Indonesia. In May 2025, the framework was upgraded to the LCT Framework covering all Balance of Payments items. Cross-border CNY settlement under LCS reached CNY 16.2 billion ($2.3B) in 2024, up 24% year-on-year. Despite this, the direct IDR-CNY market has lower liquidity than IDR-USD, meaning spreads can be wider.
How large is the PBOC-Bank Indonesia currency swap?+
CNY 400 billion (IDR 878 trillion, approximately $55.8 billion), renewed January 2025 for five years. This is one of the largest PBOC bilateral swaps globally — nearly six times larger than Thailand’s CNY 70B swap. First signed in 2009. Provides institutional RMB liquidity to Indonesian banks and promotes bilateral trade settlement in local currencies. Signed by Governor Perry Warjiyo and Governor Pan Gongsheng.
Can Wise send money from Indonesia to China?+
Yes. Unlike Vietnam (where Wise cannot send VND outbound) or Thailand (launching outbound THB in May 2026), Wise is already fully operational for outbound IDR transfers from Indonesia. You can pay from any Indonesian bank account, or via OVO and DANA e-wallets. Wise uses the mid-market exchange rate and charges approximately 1.1% in fees for IDR-to-CNY transfers. 70% of transfers arrive in under 20 seconds. Wise is regulated by Bank Indonesia.
What is the QRIS-China cross-border QR payment?+
Launched as a pilot in September 2025. UnionPay International linked Indonesia’s QRIS (40+ million merchants) with Alipay and WeChat Pay. Chinese users scan QRIS at Indonesian merchants; Indonesian users from 22 payment apps pay at pilot merchants in China. Settlement in local currencies (IDR and CNY). As of April 2026, retail payments only — not B2B trade. Full implementation expected end of 2026.
What is Flip Globe and how does it compare to Wise?+
Flip Globe is the international transfer feature of Flip, Indonesia’s largest domestic money transfer fintech. Powered by the Wise Platform, it uses Wise’s exchange rates and payment network. For China: flat IDR 76,000 admin fee with no hidden charges. Funds arrive within 24 hours. Key difference from Wise: Flip has a much larger Indonesian user base and is the default app for domestic transfers. Flat fee structure (vs. Wise’s percentage) may favor larger transfers.
What are Indonesia’s FX regulations for outbound payments?+
Managed float with no formal band. BI requires underlying transaction evidence for FX purchases exceeding $25,000 per month. Business trade payments have no hard cap with documentation. Since February 2025, natural resource exporters must retain 100% of export proceeds domestically for 12 months (Regulation No. 8 of 2025). OJK regulates banking; BI oversees monetary policy and FX. Moderately liberal by ASEAN standards — more open than Vietnam, more documentation than Thailand.
Does Bank of China have a branch in Indonesia?+
Yes. Bank of China (Hong Kong) Limited operates a Jakarta branch since 2003. Full RMB product suite: trade settlement, deposits, loans, remittances, cash exchange, FX. ICBC Indonesia and China Construction Bank Indonesia also operate — three Chinese bank options for direct RMB banking. BOC Jakarta has brought CNY 2.7 billion in banknotes to the market and processed CNY 129 billion+ in clearing in a single half-year.

Indonesia occupies a unique position: it has the most formalized institutional infrastructure of any ASEAN country for direct local currency settlement with China, yet actual adoption lags far behind capacity. The CNY 400 billion PBOC-BI swap (renewed January 2025) is nearly six times larger than Thailand’s. Eight ACCD banks operate under the LCS framework (upgraded to LCT in May 2025). Three Chinese banks serve Indonesia. Yet the vast majority of trade still settles in USD because the direct IDR-CNY market lacks depth.

This gap between infrastructure and adoption is the defining feature of this corridor. LCS settlement reached CNY 16.2 billion in 2024 — growing at 24% YoY, but a tiny fraction of ~$148 billion bilateral trade. The QRIS-China QR pilot adds retail payment integration, but B2B trade remains firmly SWIFT/USD for most businesses.

Where Indonesia genuinely excels is its fintech ecosystem. Wise operates outbound from IDR. Flip Globe (Wise Platform) reaches Indonesia’s massive domestic base. TopRemit and Transfez provide additional options. This is the richest set of regulated fintech alternatives for outbound transfers in any ASEAN corridor — a stark contrast to Vietnam (Wise cannot send VND) and Bangladesh (zero fintechs outbound). For WorldFirst, the competitive challenge is real: Wise at ~1.1% significantly undercuts WF’s ~1.9% on pure price. WF’s advantage is entirely in B2B trade features: 1688 World Pay, same-day CNAPS, World Account, and supplier payment workflows.

11 — Data Limitations Methodology notes • Next review: October 2026
  • Indonesian bank FX markups are estimated ranges based on BI reference rates (JISDOR), comparison service data, and market intelligence. Banks do not publicly disclose exact FX margin schedules. IDR-CNY spread is typically wider than IDR-USD due to lower direct market liquidity.
  • BCA’s LCS route pricing is not separately published. The estimated 2–4% range includes both SWIFT and LCS routes. ACCD banks may offer tighter spreads on LCS but this is client-negotiated.
  • BNI’s commission varies $5–$50 depending on amount and relationship, making total cost unpredictable.
  • Flip Globe pricing (IDR 76,000 flat) is confirmed from published materials. FX powered by Wise Platform but may differ slightly from Wise’s direct rates.
  • TopRemit and Transfez pricing based on promotional materials and app listings. Actual FX markup not separately disclosed.
  • BOC Jakarta pricing is the least documented. Estimates based on comparable BOC operations in other ASEAN markets.
  • LCS settlement volume (CNY 16.2 billion, 2024) from PBOC/BI announcements. Includes all bilateral LCS transactions, not just trade.
  • WorldFirst costs use maximum published FX ceiling (SMT tier). China payment fee 0.40% at high volume; standard $1–$5.
  • Currency composition (USD ~80%+, CNY ~5–8%) estimated from SWIFT data, BI statistics, and PBOC reports.
  • Data collected April 2026. The expanding LCS/LCT framework and QRIS-China QR pilot may materially alter the landscape. Next review: October 2026.
