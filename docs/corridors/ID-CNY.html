03 — Key Findings 8 findings • research grade
  • 01 Indonesia has the most developed CNY infrastructure in ASEAN. The PBOC–BI bilateral currency swap (CNY 400B / IDR 878T, renewed January 2025 for five years) is one of the PBOC's largest globally. The Local Currency Settlement framework, upgraded to the Local Currency Transaction (LCT) framework in September 2025, now covers all balance-of-payments items. Twelve Indonesian banks and eight Chinese banks serve as Appointed Cross Currency Dealers (ACCDs). LCT volume reached USD 6.23 billion in the first seven months of 2025 — 45% of Indonesia's total LCT activity.
  • 02 Indonesia is ASEAN's largest economy and China's top ASEAN trade partner. GDP of ~$1.5 trillion (2025), population 284 million, 64 million SMEs. Bilateral trade reached $135 billion in 2024. China is Indonesia's largest trading partner and has been for over a decade. Indonesia runs a persistent trade deficit with China of ~$10–15 billion annually, creating net payment demand flowing from Indonesian importers to Chinese exporters.
  • 03 Unlike Vietnam, Indonesia has a functioning fintech ecosystem for outbound payments. Flip Globe and Transfez are both BI-licensed and can send IDR to China in CNY. Wise can send from Indonesia (with restrictions — business accounts blocked, credit/debit cards blocked, monthly cap IDR 15B). Airwallex acquired a PJP Category 1 license in December 2025 and is building outbound capability. This is a competitive corridor, not a fintech desert.
  • 04 BCA offers a dedicated Yuan Remittance product via the LCS/LCT framework. BCA is an ACCD bank with direct IDR–CNY conversion, bypassing USD entirely. Telex fee: IDR 50,000. Exchange-in-lieu fee: 0.125% (min $5, max $150). Full amount fee to China: $10–25. FX markup estimated 1–2.5% on the IDR/CNY pair. Best-case all-in: ~1.75%. This is the strongest domestic bank proposition in the corridor.
  • 05 Cross-border QR payment pilot launched September 2025. UnionPay International, under PBOC and BI guidance, connected China's mobile payment apps (UnionPay App, Alipay) with Indonesia's QRIS network covering 40+ million merchants. Settlement in local currencies. Currently retail/tourism only — not available for B2B trade — but signals deepening payment infrastructure between the two economies.
  • 06 Indonesia operates a managed float — no fixed band, active intervention. Bank Indonesia intervenes in spot, domestic NDF (DNDF), and offshore NDF markets across Asia, Europe, and the US to stabilize the rupiah. BI requires documentation of underlying transactions for FX purchases exceeding USD 25,000 per month. This is less restrictive than Vietnam's +/–5% band but still imposes compliance burden on business payments.
  • 07 Indonesia's top exports to China are iron/steel, nickel, and palm oil. Iron and steel contributed 29.26% of exports to China in 2024 ($16B+). Nickel exports totaled $6.26 billion, driven by EV battery and stainless steel demand. Palm oil added $5.09 billion. These commodity flows create massive FX conversion demand — but the payment direction for WorldFirst's corridor (ID→CN, i.e. imports) is driven by Indonesia's deficit, not its exports.
  • 08 WorldFirst's 1688 World Pay is a standout differentiator despite the more competitive landscape. At 1.5% FX + 0.80% = 2.3% total, it provides direct 1688.com checkout — no Indonesian bank or local fintech offers this. For the estimated 5–10 million Indonesian businesses sourcing from 1688, this removes the need for sourcing agents (5–10% commission) and compresses the order-to-payment cycle from days to minutes.

09 — Frequently Asked Questions 8 questions • Indonesia-specific
What is the cheapest way to send money from Indonesia to China? +
WorldFirst is the cheapest documented option for business payments at approximately 1.9% total cost (up to 1.5% FX markup plus 0.40% China payment fee on the SMT tier). For personal transfers, Flip Globe and Transfez can be marginally cheaper at 1–2% but lack business-grade features. Among traditional banks, BCA's Yuan Remittance via the LCS route offers the best domestic bank pricing at ~1.75–3%, while BRI and BNI via standard SWIFT can cost up to 4.5%. The corridor is more competitive than Vietnam due to the presence of BI-licensed fintechs and the LCS/LCT framework enabling direct IDR–CNY conversion.
What is the Indonesia-China Local Currency Settlement (LCS) framework? +
The LCS framework was established by Bank Indonesia and the PBOC in September 2020, with bilateral implementation beginning September 2021. It enables direct IDR/CNY settlement for trade and investment transactions without routing through USD. In September 2025, it was upgraded to the Local Currency Transaction (LCT) framework — expanding coverage from current account and direct investment to all balance-of-payments items. Twelve Indonesian banks and eight Chinese banks serve as Appointed Cross Currency Dealers (ACCDs), including BCA, Bank Mandiri, ICBC Indonesia, Bank of China Hong Kong Jakarta, and CCB Indonesia. In the first seven months of 2025, LCT transactions reached USD 6.23 billion, about 45% of Indonesia's total LCT volume with all partners.
How large is the PBOC-Bank Indonesia currency swap? +
The Bilateral Currency Swap Arrangement (BCSA) was renewed in January 2025 for five years at CNY 400 billion / IDR 878 trillion (approximately USD 55 billion equivalent). Signed by BI Governor Perry Warjiyo and PBOC Governor Pan Gongsheng, it is one of the PBOC's largest swap lines globally. The arrangement has been in place since 2009 and has been renewed and expanded multiple times. It provides institutional CNY liquidity to Indonesia's banking system, backstops the LCS/LCT framework, and strengthens financial stability cooperation. This is a structural advantage Indonesia has over corridors like Vietnam, which lacks any PBOC swap agreement.
Can Wise send money from Indonesia to China? +
Partially. Unlike Vietnam (where outbound is completely blocked), Wise can send IDR internationally from Indonesia. However, business account transfers are explicitly prohibited, credit and debit card funding is not accepted, and the monthly cap is IDR 15 billion (approximately USD 900,000). Transfers can only be funded via Indonesian bank transfer or OVO/DANA e-wallets. Wise does not offer a direct IDR-to-CNY route — the conversion goes through USD intermediation. For B2B trade payments to Chinese suppliers, Wise is not a viable option due to the business account restriction. It may suit personal remittances under the monthly cap.
How does WorldFirst's 1688 World Pay work for Indonesian buyers? +
WorldFirst's 1688 World Pay enables direct checkout payments on 1688.com (Alibaba's domestic Chinese wholesale marketplace) from a WorldFirst account. Total cost: approximately 1.5% FX + 0.80% platform fee = 2.3%. This is uniquely valuable for Indonesian SMEs sourcing from 1688, as no Indonesian bank or local fintech offers direct 1688 checkout integration. Without World Pay, buyers must either use a sourcing agent (5–10% commission), wire payments via SWIFT (2–4 day delay, $200+ in fees), or use informal payment channels. World Pay compresses the order-to-payment cycle from days to minutes and payment arrives under the buyer's company name.
What is Indonesia's exchange rate regime? +
Indonesia operates a managed float. Unlike Vietnam's explicit +/–5% band, Bank Indonesia does not publish a formal trading band but actively intervenes to stabilize the rupiah. BI uses multiple instruments: spot market intervention, domestic non-deliverable forwards (DNDF), offshore NDF operations across Asia/Europe/US time zones, and secondary market purchases of government bonds (SBN). As of late 2025, the BI reference rate was approximately IDR 16,735 per USD. The BI policy rate stood at 4.75% through most of 2025. For FX transactions, BI requires evidence of underlying transactions for purchases exceeding USD 25,000 per month — a compliance requirement that affects all business payments.
What is the cross-border QR payment between Indonesia and China? +
In September 2025, UnionPay International launched a pilot cross-border QR payment linkage between China and Indonesia under PBOC and BI guidance. Chinese users can scan Indonesia's QRIS code at over 40 million Indonesian merchants using UnionPay App and Alipay. In parallel, Indonesian users from 22 mobile payment apps can pay at UnionPay/Alipay merchants in China. Settlement is in local currencies per the central bank agreement. Currently retail and tourism only — not available for B2B trade payments. The pilot signals deepening bilateral payment infrastructure and a potential future pathway for simplified small-value trade settlements.
How fast are payments from Indonesia to China? +
WorldFirst offers same-day CNY settlement for payments processed through China's CNAPS domestic rail. BCA's Yuan Remittance via the LCS/LCT route typically takes 1–2 business days as it avoids USD correspondent banking chains. Other Indonesian banks via standard SWIFT take 2–4 business days, depending on correspondent relationships and compliance screening. Flip Globe and Transfez claim 1–2 business days for CNY transfers. Speed is a meaningful differentiator for Indonesian importers with tight supplier payment terms — particularly when ordering from 1688 or Alibaba where supplier dispatch is often conditional on confirmed payment.

Indonesia's position in the China corridor is structurally different from other ASEAN economies. The combination of a massive PBOC swap line (CNY 400 billion), an operational LCT framework with 20 ACCD banks, and multiple BI-licensed fintechs creates a corridor with genuine competition and direct IDR–CNY liquidity. This is not a fintech desert like Vietnam — it is a developing market with improving infrastructure.

The September 2025 LCT upgrade was a milestone: expanding local currency settlement from trade and direct investment to all balance-of-payments items. Combined with the cross-border QR pilot (connecting QRIS with 40+ million merchants to China's mobile payment ecosystem), Indonesia is building the most comprehensive bilateral payment infrastructure with China of any ASEAN economy.

Indonesia's 64 million SMEs form the backbone of import demand from China. The top imports — machinery, electronics, organic chemicals, iron/steel products, and vehicles — generate steady, recurring payment flows. WorldFirst's competitive advantage here is not the absence of alternatives (as in Vietnam) but the combination of business-grade features: named beneficiary payments, same-day CNAPS settlement, and 1688 World Pay integration that no competitor matches.

11 — Data Limitations Methodology notes • Next review: October 2026
  • Bank FX markups are estimated ranges based on published indicative rates and market intelligence. Indonesian banks do not publicly disclose exact FX margin schedules — actual rates are negotiated per client relationship and transaction size. BCA's exchange-in-lieu waiver (through June 2026) may reduce the effective cost for Forex Pocket users.
  • Flip Globe and Transfez bundle FX markup and fees into a single displayed rate, making direct FX markup comparison difficult. The "1–3%" range reflects observed total cost across multiple test queries. Actual rates fluctuate with market conditions and transaction amount.
  • Wise's IDR outbound capability is confirmed but business account restrictions are based on published help documentation as of April 2026. Wise's licensing status in Indonesia has been in flux (e-wallet discontinuation, card withdrawal) — availability may change.
  • Airwallex Indonesia is listed as "not yet live" for outbound. The PJP-1 license acquisition was December 2025. Outbound payment capability may launch at any time in 2026 and would materially change the competitive landscape.
  • The LCT volume figure ($6.23 billion, Jan–Jul 2025) is from PBOC published data and covers all balance-of-payments items between Indonesia and China, not just trade payments. It is not a proxy for the retail/SME payment market that WorldFirst targets.
  • WorldFirst SMT tier pricing is based on published rate schedules. Actual fees for specific accounts may vary based on volume, account age, and product mix. The 0.40% China rate and 0.80% 1688 rate are standard published prices.
  • Cross-border QR payment is in pilot phase as of April 2026. Coverage, merchant acceptance, and settlement details may evolve. It is not currently available for B2B trade payments.
  • Data collected April 2026. Exchange rates, regulatory requirements, and provider pricing are subject to change. Next scheduled review: October 2026.
